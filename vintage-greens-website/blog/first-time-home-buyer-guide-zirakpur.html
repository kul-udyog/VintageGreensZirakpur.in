A First-Time Home Buyer's Guide to Zirakpur

Buying Guide · 7 min read

Buying your first apartment is a paperwork-heavy process, and Zirakpur's mix of established and newer developers makes due diligence especially worthwhile. Here's a practical checklist for anyone booking their first unit in the belt.

Start with RERA verification

Every legitimate residential project in Punjab must be registered with RERA (Real Estate Regulatory Authority). Before booking, look up the project's RERA number on rera.punjab.gov.in and confirm the registered developer name, project timeline, and sanctioned plan match what you've been shown. This single step protects you from a large share of the disputes that arise in Indian real estate.

Understand carpet area vs. super area

This is the single most common point of confusion for first-time buyers. Carpet area is the actual usable floor space inside your walls. Super area (sometimes called saleable area) adds a proportional share of common spaces — lobbies, staircases, corridors, clubhouse — on top of that. Pricing is typically quoted per square foot of super area, so two units with the same quoted price per sq. ft. can have meaningfully different actual living space depending on the loading factor the builder applies. Always ask for the carpet area figure explicitly and calculate your real cost per usable square foot.

Budget beyond the sale price

The quoted apartment price is rarely the full cost. Plan for:

Stamp duty and registration — varies by state and is charged on the property's registered value.
GST — applicable on under-construction properties.
Maintenance deposit — many developers collect an advance maintenance corpus at possession.
Parking and preferential location charges — often quoted separately from the base price.
Home loan processing fees — if you're financing the purchase.

Together these can add a meaningful percentage on top of the headline price, so budget for them from the start rather than discovering them at the booking stage.

Check the developer's completed track record

A developer's marketing renders tell you about their design ambitions. Their completed, delivered projects tell you whether they follow through. Ask specifically for projects the developer has finished and handed over — not just ones currently under construction — and if possible, visit one in person to see the actual build quality and how well amenities have been maintained after possession.

Questions worth asking before you book

What is the exact possession date, in writing? What happens contractually if that date slips? Is the price fixed or subject to escalation clauses? What exactly is included in the maintenance charge, and who manages it post-possession? Is home loan financing available through partner banks, and has the project been approved by major lenders? A developer who answers these clearly and in writing is generally a good sign; vague or verbal-only answers are a reason to slow down.

Visit, don't just browse

Renders and floor plans are useful for shortlisting, but a site visit tells you things a brochure can't — actual road access, noise levels, how far amenities really are from your specific tower, and construction quality on the ground.
